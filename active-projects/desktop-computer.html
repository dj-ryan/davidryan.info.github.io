Desktop Computer (THE DEFENDER)

I had the desire to build a computer during the ending years of highschool and at the beginning of college but never had the money or resources to justify the cost and time. Finally at the beginning of the summer of 2020 I felt like I was in a position to pursue this desire. I then began to design the type of system that I wanted.
Building a end-all be-all system with high end, maxed out components wouldn't make a lot of sense. First I wouldn't need this type of processing power and second because in a few short years all of those components would be outdated.

Wednesday August 5, 2020

Vertical GPU and Plant

Vertical GPU Mount
Mounted the GPU vertically to better exhibit the shroud of the MSI card. There is a possible thermal benefit with aligning the cooling fins within the card vertically however there is no proof of this yet. A small 180 degree adapter was needed for the graphics card power cable to prevent any contact with the RAM modals
Hanging plant
To finish the aesthetic of the build I hung a small plant from the venting grate on the top of the case. The plant hangs in the open space at the fornt of the case where the intake fans are located. It pulls the build together perfectly.

Tuesday June 30, 2020

FINISHED THE BUILD!

The build took about 4 hour from parts to Windows. The two biggest problems was that I forgot to power the GPU during first post and also overheating the CPU during windows install. I wanted to make sure the system would post so after installing the GPU, RAM, CUP and storage I attempted a boot. The graphics card wasn't powering on and I soon realized this was because I wasn't giving it power from the power supply. After getting graphical output I attempted to install windows. At this point I did not have the CPU cooler installed however I did not think this was going to be a problem. I was completely wrong. Installing an OS is a very CPU intensive process and the CPU would overheat not very far into the install. I only realized this was my problem by acadentily touching the CPU (and burning myself). After that I installed the CPU cooler and the rest of the process was relatively smooth. Cable management was fairly clean. The case provided several convinitly placed ports with rubber flabs to feed cables through. The only complaint that I have is there was not a direct rout for the cables to take on the back side of the motherboard around the hard drive bays and into the PSU area. However, after it was all said and down my inner OCD was very pleased. I tuned all of the RGB to a white output which really pulled the build together and gave it that clean look that I was after. An unintentional byproduct was the reflection of RAM's white LEDs from the metal backing on the GPU. It actually splits the spectrum into RGB which looks pretty cool. I am extremely happy with the final product!

Thursday June 25, 2020

Motherbaord & storage purchases

Gigabyte B550 AORUS ELITE ATX AM4 Motherboard
The motherboard choice came more out of necessity rather then preference. The great COVID-19 shortage really did a number on the PC component market. Nevertheless I am pleased with the it. Its a B550 model which were just released and had plenty of IO ports for my needs including USB 3.0 Gen 2. The chipset was designed with Ryzon 3 in mind and would not need a BIOS updated which might be needed in the earlier B450 models. I also like the subtle RGB accenting next to the IO ports and audio drivers.
Western Digital Blue 1 TB M.2-2280 Solid State Drive
Originally I was going to go for a 1TB SSD with a SATA connection bu the upgrade to a M.2 was minimal and the performance boost was worth it.

Wednesday June 24, 2020

Monitor purchases

Asus TUF GAMING VG249Q 23.8" 1920x1080 144 Hz Monitor
I was definitely not planning on getting this expensive of a monitor but I am glad that I did. I knew I wanted to purchases a IPS monitor because I wanted to be able to turn in into a vertical monitor with the longe term goal of having a multi monitor set up. However this limitation did not alow me to get a high refresh rate and high response time monitor in my price range. So I eventually decided to spend more and purchases this one which fit my need perfectly.

Monday June 22, 2020

Graphics card purchases

MSI GeForce RTX 2060 6 GB GAMING Z Video Card
I was teetering between the Ryzen 5700 and RTX 2060. I Finally decided on RTX 2060 because I wanted Ray Tracing and GeForce has reliable and feature filled drivers. The 5700 definitely has better performance but I was willing to do the trade off for the aforementioned benefits. It also included some RGB which was an added bonus and would help compleat my white led build. This card is also on the lower end of the midrange cards which gives me room to upgrade in the future.

Thursday June 18, 2020

CPU purchases

AMD Ryzen 5 3600X 3.8 GHz 6-Core Processor
I was planning on getting the Ryzen 5 3600 but a friend was upgrading his system and needed to sell his 3600x so I got that for a bargain. The only difference between the two CPUs is the base clock speed with the 3600 being 3.6 GHz (4.2 boost) and the 3600x being 3.8 GHz (4.4 boost).

Tuesday June 16, 2020

Purchased the case, RAM and extra fans

Corsair 275R ATX Mid Tower Case
I knew that I wanted something minimal from Corsair that provided good air flow. They delivered with this guy. The air flow is great with the uninterrupted side channels on the front for air input and the grill on the top of the system for hot air output. I especially love the LEDs on the bottom that give it a polished look.
Corsair Vengeance RGB Pro 16 GB (2 x 8 GB) DDR4-3200 CL16 Memory
The RAM is pretty basic with dual chanel (8x2) clocked at 3200 MHz.
Corsair AF120 LED (2018) White 52 CFM 120 mm Fans 3-Pack
The extra fans are to fill the extra slots on the case. The case only comes with two fans.

Thursday June 11, 2020

Purchased Power Supply

EVGA B5 650 W 80+ Bronze Certified Fully Modular ATX Power Supply
650 watts was plenty for this system and allowed some overhead for overclocking if I wanted to do that in the future. I was not worried about the efficiency rating because it wasn't worth the extra cost. EVGA is known to be a reliable PSU manufacture so I favored them in this decision.
